Lord's Ground refers to Lord's Cricket Ground, a historic and iconic cricket venue located in St John's Wood, London, England. Established in 1814 and owned by the Marylebone Cricket Club (MCC), it is widely known as the "Home of Cricket" and is famous for its unique playing field slope, the original Ashes urn, and its blend of Victorian heritage and modern innovation.

Narendra Modi Stadium is a cricket ground in Motera, Ahmedabad, Gujarat, India. With a total capacity of 132,000,[12] it is the world's largest cricket stadium,[13][14] It is owned by the Gujarat Cricket Association, and hosts both domestic and international cricket matches.[2][15][16] The stadium has hosted several high-profile matches, such as the 2023 One Day International World Cup final.[17] It is named after the Prime Minister of India, Narendra Modi.

Sydney Cricket Ground (SCG) is a sports stadium located in Moore Park, Sydney, Australia. It is one of the oldest cricket grounds in the world, with a history dating back to 1848. The SCG is known for its iconic Members Pavilion and the famous Sydney Cricket Ground Trust, which oversees its management. In addition to cricket, the SCG hosts Australian rules football, rugby league, and other sporting events.

Dharamshala Cricket Stadium, officially known as Himachal Pradesh Cricket Association Stadium, is a cricket stadium located in Dharamshala, Himachal Pradesh, India. It is situated at an altitude of 1,457 meters (4,780 ft) and is known for its stunning views of the Dhauladhar mountain range. The stadium was inaugurated in 2003 and has a seating capacity of 23,000 spectators. It is home to the Himachal Pradesh cricket team and has hosted several domestic and international matches.
